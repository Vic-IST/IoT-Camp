# Setting Up 20 Google Accounts for Camp Students
**MGCCC Camp 2026 — Instructor Reference**

---

## OPTION 1 (Best): Ask MGCCC IT to Provision Accounts (Recommended)

MGCCC almost certainly has **Google Workspace for Education**, which means IT can create 20 temporary student accounts in bulk — no phone numbers needed, and you control the passwords.

### What to tell IT:

> "I'm running a summer camp and need 20 temporary Google/Gmail accounts for student use. We're a Google Workspace for Education institution. Can you bulk-create accounts like `camp2026.student01@mgccc.edu` through `camp2026.student20@mgccc.edu` with a shared temporary password?"

### What IT will do (if they say yes):
1. Log into the Google Admin Console at admin.google.com
2. Go to **Directory → Users → Bulk upload users**
3. Upload a CSV with the 20 usernames and a temporary password
4. Send you the credentials

**This is the fastest and most reliable path.** All 20 accounts are tied to your school domain, no phone numbers required.

## OPTION 2 (DIY): Create Gmail Accounts on an Android Device

Creating accounts through **Android device Settings** (not a browser) bypasses phone verification most reliably.

### What you need:
- Any Android phone or tablet
- A Wi-Fi connection
- About 5–7 minutes per account

### Steps (repeat 20 times):

1. Open **Settings** on the Android device
2. Scroll to **Accounts** (or "Passwords & accounts")
3. Tap **Add account**
4. Tap **Google**
5. Tap **Create account** → choose **For my personal use** (or **For a child under 13** is NOT recommended — those have heavy restrictions)
6. Enter:
   - **First name:** Camp (or student name)
   - **Last name:** Student01 (use 01–20)
7. Enter **date of birth** — use an adult age (18+) to avoid child account restrictions
8. Choose a Gmail address — suggested format: `mgccccamp2026s01@gmail.com`
9. Create a **password** (use one standard password for all, e.g., `CampMGCCC2026!`)
10. When prompted for a phone number, look for a **"Skip"** option — tap it
    - If Skip does not appear, proceed to Option 3 below
11. Add a **recovery email** (use your own: vic.gauthreaux@mgccc.edu) — this is optional but helpful
12. Accept terms and finish

### Tips to avoid phone verification prompts:
- Switch to a different Wi-Fi network after every 3–4 accounts (Google flags repeated signups from the same IP)
- Clear the Google app cache between accounts if using the same device
- Use a different device for every 4–5 accounts if you have access to multiple

## OPTION 3 (Backup): Use a Browser on Desktop with Skip

Some setups still allow skipping phone verification via browser.

1. Open **Chrome in Incognito mode** (Ctrl+Shift+N)
2. Go to [accounts.google.com/signup](https://accounts.google.com/signup)
3. Fill in name and username (follow naming convention above)
4. At the phone number screen, look for **"Skip"** — if present, click it
5. Complete setup

> **Note:** If Google does not show Skip, your IP address has been flagged for too many recent signups. Switch networks (e.g., mobile hotspot) and try again.

---

## OPTION 4 (Last Resort): Virtual Phone Number via TextNow

If phone verification is unavoidable, use **TextNow** to get free virtual numbers.

> ⚠️ TextNow numbers are VoIP numbers. Google may reject them. This method is less reliable in 2026.

1. Download **TextNow** (free) on a smartphone
2. Create a TextNow account — you get a free phone number
3. Use that number for Google SMS verification
4. After the code is received, the number can be reused for the next account (some accounts, not all)
5. If Google rejects it, try a different number by creating another free TextNow account

## Important Notes

- **Do this before camp starts** — Google sometimes temporarily locks new accounts for 24 hours after creation. Create all accounts at least 2–3 days in advance and log into each one at least once so they're "warmed up."
- **Log into Google Colab once** from each account before camp so students aren't blocked by first-time access screens during your session.
- **Save all credentials** somewhere secure (a printed sheet works fine for a short camp).
- **After camp ends**, you can leave the accounts as-is or simply stop using them. They won't cause any issues.
